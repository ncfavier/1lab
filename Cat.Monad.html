Monadsüîó

A monad on a category C\ca{C}C is one way of categorifying the concept of monoid. Specifically, rather than living in a monoidal category, a monad lives in a bicategory. Here, we concern ourselves with the case of monads in the bicategory of categories, so that we may say: A monad is an endofunctor MMM, equipped with a unit natural transformation Id‚áíM\mathrm{Id} \To MId‚áíM, and a multiplication (M‚àòM)‚áíM(M \circ M) \To M(M‚àòM)‚áíM.

Algebras over a monadüîó

One way of interpreting a monad MMM is as giving a signature for an algebraic theory. For instance, the free monoid monad describes the signature for the theory of monoids, and the free group monad describes the theory of groups.

Under this light, an algebra over a monad is a way of evaluating the abstract operations given by a monadic expression to a concrete value. Formally, an algebra for MMM is given by a choice of object AAA and a morphism ŒΩ:M(A)‚ÜíA\nu : M(A) \to AŒΩ:M(A)‚ÜíA.

record AlgebraOn (M : Monad) (ob : C.Ob) : Type (o ‚äî h) where
  no-eta-equality
  open Monad M

  field
    ŒΩ : C.Hom (M‚ÇÄ ob) ob

This morphism must satisfy equations categorifying those which define a monoid action. If we think of MMM as specifying a signature of effects, then v-unit says that the unit has no effects, and v-mult says that, given two layers M(M(A))M(M(A))M(M(A)), it doesn‚Äôt matter whether you first join then evaluate, or evaluate twice.

    ŒΩ-mult : ŒΩ C.‚àò M‚ÇÅ ŒΩ ‚â° ŒΩ C.‚àò mult.Œ∑ ob

Algebra : Monad ‚Üí Type (o ‚äî h)
Algebra M = Œ£ (AlgebraOn M)

Eilenberg-Moore Categoryüîó

There is a natural definition of MMM-algebra homomorphism: It is a map of the underlying objects of each algebra which commutes with the evaluation operation.

record AlgebraHom (M : Monad) (X Y : Algebra M) : Type (o ‚äî h) where
  no-eta-equality
  private
    module X = AlgebraOn (X .snd)
    module Y = AlgebraOn (Y .snd)

  field

Since commutes is an inhabitant of a proposition, it suffices to test equality of the underlying morphisms to conclude that two algebra homomorphisms are equal.

Free Algebrasüîó

For every object AAA, there is a free MMM-algebra, where the underlying object is M(A)M(A)M(A) and the action is multiplication.
