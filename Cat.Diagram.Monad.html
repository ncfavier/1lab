Monadsüîó

A monad on a category C\ca{C}C is one way of categorifying the concept of monoid. Specifically, rather than living in a monoidal category, a monad lives in a bicategory. Here, we concern ourselves with the case of monads in the bicategory of categories, so that we may say: A monad is an endofunctor MMM, equipped with a unit natural transformation Id‚áíM\id{Id} \To MId‚áíM, and a multiplication (M‚àòM)‚áíM(M \circ M) \To M(M‚àòM)‚áíM.

Algebras over a monadüîó

One way of interpreting a monad MMM is as giving a signature for an algebraic theory. For instance, the free monoid monad describes the signature for the theory of monoids, and the free group monad describes the theory of groups.

Under this light, an algebra over a monad is a way of evaluating the abstract operations given by a monadic expression to a concrete value. Formally, an algebra for MMM is given by a choice of object AAA and a morphism ŒΩ:M(A)‚ÜíA\nu : M(A) \to AŒΩ:M(A)‚ÜíA.

  no-eta-equality
  open Monad M

  field
    ŒΩ : C.Hom (M‚ÇÄ ob) ob

This morphism must satisfy equations categorifying those which define a monoid action. If we think of MMM as specifying a signature of effects, then v-unit says that the unit has no effects, and v-mult says that, given two layers M(M(A))M(M(A))M(M(A)), it doesn‚Äôt matter whether you first join then evaluate, or evaluate twice.

    ŒΩ-mult : ŒΩ C.‚àò M‚ÇÅ ŒΩ ‚â° ŒΩ C.‚àò mult.Œ∑ ob

Eilenberg-Moore Categoryüîó

If we take a monad MMM as the signature of an (algebraic) theory, and MMM-algebras as giving models of that theory, then we can ask (like with everything in category theory): Are there maps between interpretations? The answer (as always!) is yes: An algebra homomorphism is a map of the underlying objects which ‚Äúcommutes with the algebras‚Äù.

record Algebra-hom (M : Monad) (X Y : Algebra M) : Type (o ‚äî h) where

  private
    module X = Algebra-on (X .snd)
    module Y = Algebra-on (Y .snd)

Since the square we drew above commutes for the identity morphism, and we can show that the composite of two algebra homomorphisms is an algebra homomorphism, they assemble into a category: The Eilenberg-Moore category of MMM.

Free Algebrasüîó

In exactly the same way that we may construct a free group by taking the inhabitants of some set XXX as generating the ‚Äúwords‚Äù of a group, we can, given an object AAA of the underlying category, build a free MMM-algebra on AAA. Keeping with our interpretation of monads as logical signatures, this is the syntactic model of MMM, with a set of ‚Äúneutrals‚Äù chosen from the object AAA.

This construction is a lot simpler to do in generality than in any specific case: We can always turn AAA into an MMM-algebra by taking the underlying object to be M(A)M(A)M(A), and the algebra map to be the monadic multiplication; The associativity and unit laws of the monad itself become those of the MMM-action.

  Free : Functor C Eilenberg-Moore

    record
      { ŒΩ      = mult.Œ∑ A
      ; ŒΩ-mult = mult-assoc
      ; ŒΩ-unit = right-ident
      }
