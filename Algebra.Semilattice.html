Semilatticesüîó

A semilattice is a partially ordered set where all elements have a meet (a meet semilattice), or a join (a join semilattice). Rather than use a definition in terms of ordered sets, we choose an algebraic definition of semilattices: A semilattice (A,‚àß)(A, \land)(A,‚àß) is a commutative semigroup where every element is idempotent: x‚àßx=xx \land x = xx‚àßx=x.

As is typical with algebraic structures, we define a semilattice homomorphism as being a map which commutes with the binary operator. Since being a semilattice is a property of (A,‚àß)(A, \land)(A,‚àß), we have a characterisation of identifications of semilattices: Two semilattices are identified precisely when their underlying types are equivalent by some homomorphic equivalence.
